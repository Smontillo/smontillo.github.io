Sebastian received his B.S. in Chemistry in 2020 from the Universidad de Antioquia, Colombia, under the supervision of Prof. Albeiro Restrepo. He investigated the absorption of ibuprofen into lipid bilayers by developing Python libraries that interfaced molecular-mechanics software (GROMACS) with electronic-structure packages (Gaussian). This QM/MM framework enabled the characterization of ibuprofen’s bonding evolution during membrane insertion and the analysis of its spectroscopic signatures.

He then moved to the University of Rochester to pursue his Ph.D. under the supervision of Prof. Frank (Pengfei) Huo. His research focuses on understanding how strong light–matter interactions modify chemical reactivity by employing open-quantum-systems simulation frameworks, developing mixed quantum–classical (MQC) dynamics methods, and formulating rate theories that capture these reactivity changes. He has also collaborated closely with experimental groups at the University of Texas at Austin to interpret and explain their observations.
